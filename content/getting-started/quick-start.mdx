# Quick Start

Get a personal AI assistant running in about five minutes. Use the Personal Agent template from the marketplace and connect your tools.

**Prerequisites:** A Scout account (free plan available)

---

## Step 1: Get the Template

The fastest way to get started is with a pre-built template.

1. Open [Scout Studio](https://studio.scoutos.com)
2. Click **Templates** or **Marketplace** in the sidebar
3. Find the **Personal Agent** template
4. Click **Use Template**

Give your agent a name (like "My Assistant") and you're ready to configure it.

---

## Step 2: Connect Your Calendar

Your agent can check your schedule, find meeting times, and remind you of upcoming events.

1. Go to **Settings** → **Integrations**
2. Click **Connect** next to Google Calendar or Outlook
3. Authorize the connection
4. Return to your agent and make sure the calendar tool is enabled

**Try it:** Ask your agent "What's on my calendar today?"

---

## Step 3: Connect Your Email

Let your agent read, summarize, and draft emails for you.

1. Go to **Settings** → **Integrations**
2. Click **Connect** next to Gmail or Outlook
3. Authorize the connection
4. Enable the email tool in your agent

**Try it:** Ask "Summarize my unread emails from today"

---

## Step 4: Choose Your Chat Platform

Talk to your agent where you already work—Slack or Telegram.

### Slack

1. Go to **Integrations** → **Connect** Slack
2. Select your workspace and authorize
3. Choose which channels your agent can access

### Telegram

1. Go to **Integrations** → **Connect** Telegram
2. Start a chat with the Scout bot
3. Follow the authorization steps

**Try it:** Send "Remind me in 30 minutes to take a break"

---

## Next Steps

Your personal agent is ready. Here's how to get more from it:

**Schedule daily briefings** — Have your agent send you a morning summary with calendar events, urgent emails, and priorities. Go to **Settings** → **Schedule** to set this up.

**Customize instructions** — Edit your agent's instructions to change its tone, focus on specific tasks, or be more proactive.

**Add more tools** — Connect Notion for notes, GitHub for issues, or Salesforce for pipeline tracking.

---

## Tips

- **Start simple** — Add tools one at a time so you can test each connection
- **Be specific** — Clearer instructions = better results
- **Chat regularly** — The more you use it, the better it gets
